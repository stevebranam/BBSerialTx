void BBSerialTx_Calibrate ( void  )

Outputs a calibration pattern on the BBSerialTx channel.

The pattern can be measured with a logic analyzer or oscilloscope. It consists of a preamble of 1000 writes of idle values (logic 1), 1000 writes of alternating 1's and 0's computed from a known input byte, and a postamble of 1000 idles. This makes it easy to isolate on the analyzer trace.

Measure the width of the alternating region in usec and divide the time by 1000 to get the number of nsec per write; pass this value to the BBSerialTx_Open() function as the write_duration_nsec argument.

The write operation consists of both computation for the bits of the byte, and GPIO hardware control. Depending on the processor architecture, the calculated nsec value may be very precise, or it may be coarse. You may need to adjust the value by up to 5% to get more accurate baud rate control and avoid framing errors.

Calibration is typically very platform-specific. A different MCU or board design may require a different calibration value.
